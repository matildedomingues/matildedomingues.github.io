Normal is not normal

We live in a world where young people, in particular, are exposed to almost unattainable standards of beauty and perfection, essentially spread by social networks that glorify the ideal. However, one thing is certain: there are no normal bodies or beauty ideals. We must accept our scars, moles, blemishes and all the parts of our bodies that we often don't like. These features are visible narratives of our human journey, evidence that we have grown, fallen and continue to evolve.

Each mark on our body tells a part of our story. They can be unhappy memories from our childhood, the results of acts of courage, or simply characteristics that distinguish us and that may have been born with us. Accepting these characteristics is accepting our history as it is, without reproach. When we decide to love every part of ourselves, we are redefining the concept of beauty to include authenticity and individuality.

It's essential to recognize that what are often seen as imperfections are actually what make us unique. And, where so many still seek external acceptance, it's crucial to find the courage to say: we are enough exactly as we are.

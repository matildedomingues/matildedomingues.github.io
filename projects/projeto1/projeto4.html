Yourself

Each mark on our body tells a part of our story. It can either be an unfortunate memory from our childhood, the result of an act of courage at some point, or simply a characteristic that sets us apart and may even have been born with us. To accept these characteristics is to accept our history as it is, without reproach. Therefore, we must look at all of this and not see it as imperfections, but rather recognise that it is what makes us unique.

* Video project carried out through real stories and testimonies.

We live in a world in which young people, in particular, are exposed to almost unattainable standards of beauty and perfection, essentially spread by social networks that glorify the ideal. But one thing is certain: we should embrace our scars, our moles, blemishes and all the parts of our body that we often don't like, because they are visible narratives of our human journey.

They are proof that we have grown, fallen and continue to evolve. In the immensity of this world, where so many still seek external acceptance, may we find the courage to say: we are enough exactly as we are.
